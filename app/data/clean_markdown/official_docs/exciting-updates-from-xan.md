## Navigating Exchanges
Bernie recently discussed the evolving world of exchanges, including centralized (CEX) and decentralized (DEX) platforms. The lines are blurring with hybrids that combine the best of both worlds.

### What Are Centralized and Decentralized Exchanges?
* Centralized Exchanges (CEX): traditional marketplaces run by a single company, offering fast and user-friendly transactions
* Decentralized Exchanges (DEX): run on smart contracts deployed on blockchains, offering non-custodial and transparent transactions

### Pros and Cons at a Glance
For CEX:
* Pros: lightning-fast trades, polished interfaces, and customer support
* Cons: custodial, vulnerable to hacks or regulations, and goes against the "own your keys" spirit of crypto

For DEX:
* Pros: full control over assets, integrates well with DeFi, and transparent code
* Cons: steeper learning curve, sometimes lower liquidity, and more complex to use

## Why This Matters for Xandeum and the XAND Token
The XAND token is currently available on DEXes, aligning with Xandeum's decentralized ethos. However, CEX listings will be crucial for the next 3 to 5 years, offering massive user bases, strong marketing, and the ability to build excitement.
