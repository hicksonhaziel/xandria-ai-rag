## Introduction to Xandeum's Six Eras of Innovation
Xandeum has taken a unique approach to development, focusing on six distinct eras of innovation rather than a traditional roadmap. These eras are designed to guide the project's growth and development, with a total of 30 planned releases.

## The Six Eras of Innovation
The six eras are:
1. Deep South
2. South
3. Mine
4. Central
5. Coal
6. North

Each era has its own set of goals and objectives, which are outlined on the Xandeum website.

## The Deep South Era
The Deep South era is the first stage of development, consisting of two releases: the Constant release and the Fryberg release. This era is focused on getting started, with the registration of provider nodes (P nodes) that will store data.

## The South Era
The South era begins with the Munich release, which is the first working prototype. This era consists of seven releases, with the goal of developing a minimum viable product (MVP). The Munich release will be deployed on a test net, and P node operators will receive real fees for storing data.

## The Mine Era
The Mine era starts with the Frankfurt release, which marks the transition to a decentralized version of Wikipedia. During this era, the storage will be orchestrated in a centralized manner to facilitate quick time-to-market.

## The Central Era
The Central era is focused on decentralization, with the goal of making the storage layer self-sufficient. This era will introduce Byzantine fault tolerance, which will enable the P nodes to administer themselves and ensure the integrity of the data. The Central era will also feature trust anchoring to other blockchains, providing an additional layer of security.

## The Coal Era
The Coal era begins with the Essen release, which introduces fee markets. This era is focused on creating a market-oriented system, where supply and demand dictate the prices for storage. The goal is to create a system that is responsive to changes in demand, with the ability to scale quickly to meet the needs of users.

## The North Era
The North era is the final stage of development, starting with the Moer release. This era is focused on scalability and performance, with the goal of optimizing the system for high transaction volumes and large numbers of P nodes.

## Timeline and Goals
The goal is to complete all 30 releases within 2025, with a focus on rapid development and deployment. The team is working on multiple releases in parallel, with the Constant release and Fryberg release already in progress. The Munich release is scheduled for the summer, with the goal of deploying a working prototype on a test net.
