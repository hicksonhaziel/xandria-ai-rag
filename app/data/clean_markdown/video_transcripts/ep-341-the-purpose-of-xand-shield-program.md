## Introduction to Xandum Storage Layer
The xandum storage layer is a crucial component of web 3, enabling decentralized apps to store data efficiently. The current architecture of smart contract chains, including Solana, relies on validator nodes to store data, resulting in high redundancy and limited scalability.

## The Role of P-Nodes
P-Nodes, or provider nodes, are specialized nodes that provide storage for the xandum storage layer. They are designed to be smaller and more efficient than validator nodes, requiring only storage and a reliable internet connection. P-Nodes will be the core of the xandum network, with 94% of fees generated on the network going to these nodes.

## Why P-Nodes are Necessary
P-Nodes are necessary to reduce redundancy and increase scalability. With the current architecture, every piece of data is stored on 3,000 validators, resulting in high costs and limited capacity. P-Nodes will allow apps to decide how much redundancy they need, enabling more efficient storage and reduced costs.

## Community Aspect
The xandum community is excited about the opportunity to run P-Nodes, with many individuals eager to participate. The xandum team wants to diversify node ownership, limiting the number of nodes per wallet to ensure a spread-out distribution among the community.

## Cost Impact
The use of P-Nodes will significantly reduce costs compared to traditional infrastructure. By bringing redundancy down from 3,000 to 3, for example, costs will be reduced by 1,000 times. Additionally, lower hardware requirements will further reduce costs.

## Pricing Model
The pricing model for P-Nodes will be community-driven and decentralized, with fee markets balancing supply and demand. The xandum team envisions a market-driven fee model, where middlemen can sell futures contracts to provide predictability for users.

## Future Plans
The xandum team plans to introduce regions, encryption layers, and other features to support a wide range of apps. The first 300 P-Nodes will become available on March 25th, with a limited number of nodes available in the first era. The team aims to make the process of running P-Nodes easy and user-friendly, with a graphical user interface and simple setup process.

## Global Expansion
The xandum team plans to expand globally, with regions and different fee models for different regions. This will enable the storage layer to support a wide range of apps and use cases, from small-scale storage to large-scale enterprise applications.

## User-Friendly Interface
The xandum team has designed a user-friendly interface, xan minor, which allows users to easily set up and manage their P-Nodes. The interface includes features such as a slider to adjust storage contribution, making it easy for non-technical users to participate.
